Social Hero

Mini project at University of California, Santa Cruz

This was a mini project during 'Intro to Design Methods' class at UCSC

Project length: ~2 weeks

Team members: 5 (Franky Dominguez, Naomi Du, Sakshi Shah, Siddharth Subramanian, Yi Qing Khoo(me))

Task: Design something based on a selected community (Community with social anxiety/would like to build social skills)

Results: A mobile game called Social Hero that helps to foster social courage through daily challenges and in-app social interactions.

My main role: 1 out 3 members in-charge of prototyping/design.

My Accomplishments

  • Created multiple prototypes ranging from low fidelity sketches to high fidelity interactable figma prototypes
  • Created the high-fidelity prototypes for 'Tavern', 'Shop', 'Academy' and 'Friends list'
  • Conducted a few user testing with classmates to identify potential pain points of the prototypes
  • Looked into similar apps currently available in app stores as part of competitor analysis

Challenges Faced

  • Features of the game

When creating the low-fidelity sketches, the prototype team were unsure of what features should be included in the design. We had discussions such as whether to include global interaction or how progress should be tracked. After listening to our team which had conducted the user interviews, it affirmed our idea of having a platform for all users to interact as well as changing a leveling system to a categorical tracker.

  • Navigating the prototype

During our usability testing, we came to realise that it was not intuitive to the users on how the map could be navigated. Many had assumed it was a tap to move avatar to the location. To better assist users in how to navigate the map, we decided to create an information board which appears when the user first uses the application. It can later be accessed for the 'i' icon on the top right of the map.

Further work on...

  • Speaking to a psychologist/professional

This will allow us to have a better understanding what types of quest can truly help foster social courage as the current quest in the prototype may not actually help with fostering social courage. Speaking to a professional can also allows us to better phrase or structure areas of our game.

  • Conducting for user interviews

Given the time constraint, the team was only able to interview schoolmates for our community user interviews. This may have limited our views on what should be included in the game. Conducting for interviews of a more diverse population will allow us to better understand the community from different perspectives.
